Iterative Design

Design an interactive for a startup

We worked in a group of four to design an interactive interface for a startup named Dropezy, a grocery delivery application in Indonesia. We started by scratching, extensively explored alternative designs, created an interactive APP interface, and conducted user testing to finalize our design.

Part 1: Sketching & Wireframing

1.1 Pre-Design Thinking

  • About The App

    • Dropezy is a grocery delivery mobile application in Indonesia that aims to provide fast, convenient, affordable, and accessible delivery services.
    • Dropezy differs from other shopping platforms in that it offers users an easy and convenient way to purchase daily needs online in smaller quantities, more frequently, and anytime.
    • Besides lightning-fast deliveries to our customers, Dropezy cares about the safety, health, and happiness of their community.
    • Dropezy embraced transparency and is committed to workplace safety and equal opportunity for women without a gender pay gap.


  • About The Interface

    Our design aims to demonstrate the concepts of Dropezy, targeting a young, affluent group of people who live in urban and suburban areas, with regular online shopping habits and relatively high consumption, who are more receptive to online shopping. In section 4, we will present how our final design aligns with the company's concepts and helps the company achieve its original goals.


1.2 Sketching

Each group member comes up with one possible design for Dropezy’s app. Our sketching is shown below. The resulting design is diverse and substantially different, especially since every version has its priority.

1.2.1 Member A's design

I believe that for shopping applications, images are more appealing to the users, so I mainly included visual elements on the main page I designed. I have a search bar on the main page so that users can go directly to the item with the search bar if they want to buy a specific item. Otherwise, users can find more things on the category page.


For the account page, I included a section for saving items or purchased items, which would be helpful for users wishing to find what they previously bought. The account page also provides a list that contains settings such as payments, languages, or delivery addresses.


I had plus and minus signs for users to change the number of items to purchase for the cart page. I also display detailed billing information, including shipping and taxes so that users can keep track of costs.


Finally, I want to highlight that for a shopping application. I included many sections for advertisements and promotions.

1.2.2 Member B's design

In my design, I think the bottom navigation bar should display which page users are currently at. So I try to distinguish the selected/not selected state using different color icons. On the home page, a search bar can help users quickly locate the item they are searching for.


The products are all under subcategories so that users can first find the category and check for the things they want efficiently. In the category page, I add a filter that can help users filter out the things they don’t like.


The “Mine” page is just a basic version of the mine page where users can browse their past orders and search for help from the agency if they find an issue with their order and want to return their items.

1.2.3 Member C's Design

My priority is to lead the user to choose and purchase, so I include all the selections on the home page, along with best sellers and seasonal offers. Also, the cart must be simple, straightforward, and cover all the essential information. Creating a list of liked items can also save time for faster and more repeatable service.

1.2.4 Member D's design

Every user has different needs. Users who purchase the same things over and over again can go to the past order page. Users can change their minds at the last moment and checkout without deleting some items they might want to get later. Users can purchase items from different stores and quickly trace their orders. Also, I think a recommendation section can better connect the Dropezy community.

1.3 Combining Ideas into a Wireframe

Based on the concepts of the company, we collect good features from all versions and carry out our final wireframe.
Efficiency:

Our final wireframe provides users with a fast shopping process. All pages allow users to add items to their cart or favorites lists, and the home, category, and cart screens provide personalized recommendations to assist them in making purchases.

Diversity:

We add social features (community) to the original shopping app, allowing users to choose-buy-cook from one app, increasing customer stickiness.

Part 4:Justify our design decisions

Our design strives to achieve the company's original goals, making Dropezy a convenient and accessible delivery tool and a community that cares. The cart page clearly layouts the taxes, shipping costs, and total prices, which guarantees transparency. We designed a favorite list, which allows users to save time and effort finding the item they want. A community feature allows users to share their shopping experience, find good recipes from influencers and discuss the hottest topics. Thus, Dropezy can better hear the voice of the users, select its products, and develop strategies to meet their daily needs.
